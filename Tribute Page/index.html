Dr. B.R. Ambedkar

Social reformer, jurist, economist, and politician who inspired the Dalit Buddhist movement and fought against social discrimination.

About the Legend

☛ Dr. Bhim Rao Ambedkar, a fighter in the cause of Annihilation of Caste, was born in a low-caste Mahar family on April 14, 1891, at Mhow cantonment, where his father served in the Army.

☛ Despite being born in a caste considered the lowest of the low, he framed the Constitution of India.

☛ His life was one of struggles, facing overt hostility from upper castes due to his radical proposals to deal with the caste system.

☛ He earned a Doctorate in Economics from Columbia University in 1917, overcoming immense social odds.

☛ The boy who suffered bitter caste humiliation became the first Minister for Law in free India and shaped the country’s Constitution.

☛ Dr. Ambedkar's struggle against Brahmanical Patriarchy and his proposals for the Hindu Code Bill and restructuring of property relations highlighted his challenge to the status quo.

☛ He transformed the social and political landscape by making the downtrodden aware of their own situation during the struggle against British colonialism.

☛ Ambedkar famously urged Dalits to “Educate, Agitate, and Organise,” emphasizing education as a tool for empowerment.

☛ He believed education was more important than temple entry for the downtrodden and saw it as a means to build an egalitarian society and break discriminatory social practices.
